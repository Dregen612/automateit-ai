## Chapter 1: Lead Follow-Up (3-5 Hours/Week)

### Why This Is Your #1 Priority
The average business responds to a lead in 47 hours. Your competitor responds in 2 minutes. AI doesn't sleep, eat lunch, or forget to follow up.

### The 2-Minute Rule
Every time a lead comes in, paste their message into this prompt:

```
You are my sales assistant. A new lead just came in:
- Name: [NAME]
- Source: [WHERE THEY CAME FROM]
- Their question: [WHAT THEY ASKED]

Write 3 email options — professional, friendly, under 100 words each.
```

### The Hot Lead Alert
Set a daily scan of your inbox. AI flags every lead that:
- Mentions budget ($X or "we have budget")
- Has a specific timeline ("need this by...")
- Comes from a referral

These get responded to first. Everything else gets batched.

### The Cold Lead Nurture
For leads who went quiet: a 5-email sequence that adds value before it asks for anything.
Email 1: Useful content (not about you)
Email 2: A relevant insight
Email 3: A question (start a conversation)
Email 4: Soft offer
Email 5: Clear next step

## Chapter 2: Email Management (2-4 Hours/Week)

### Morning Inbox Triage
Every morning, paste your unread emails into this:

```
Categorize each email:
🔴 URGENT — respond today
📅 TODAY — handle today
📆 THIS WEEK — respond when you have time
📬 ARCHIVE — no action needed

For the top 3 urgent ones, tell me what I should say.
```

This takes 2 minutes. Saves 45 minutes of scrolling.

### Response Drafting
Forward every email you need to respond to. AI drafts the reply. You review and send. Takes 5 seconds to forward, 30 seconds to review.

### The Unsubscriber Analysis
When someone unsubscribes, AI analyzes: was it the frequency? The content? The timing?
```
An unsubscribe from [email]. Their history: [open rate, last 3 emails opened].
What went wrong?
```

### Email to Text
Long email → paste into AI → "Convert this to 3 short texts. Under 160 characters each."
Now your follow-up is a text message, not a formal email.

## Chapter 3: Content Creation (2-3 Hours/Week)

### The 10-Minute Content Sprint
Every Sunday evening, spend 10 minutes generating a full week of posts:

```
I'm a [YOUR ROLE]. Topic this week: [ONE TOPIC].
Generate 7 posts — one for each day.
Twitter: under 280 chars, hook + value + question.
LinkedIn: 150-300 words, professional but personal.
```

### The Thread Generator
Have an opinion? Turn it into an 8-tweet thread. Each tweet = one idea, builds the argument, ends mid-thought to pull them to the next one.

### Repurpose Everything
Write one blog post. AI turns it into:
- 8-tweet Twitter thread
- LinkedIn post
- 5 quote graphics
- Email newsletter
- 60-second video script

One piece of content. Twelve outputs.

## Chapter 4: Scheduling & Appointments (1-2 Hours/Week)

### The Confirmation Sequence
When someone books: AI sends a confirmation that includes:
- Appointment details
- What to prepare
- How to reschedule easily (reduces no-shows by 40%)

### The Reminder Stack
- 3 days before: "Here's what to prepare"
- 1 day before: "See you tomorrow at [time]"
- 1 hour before: "Ready? Here's the link"

### Post-Appointment Follow-Up
After every call, AI drafts a follow-up email with:
- What was agreed
- Next steps (who does what)
- When you'll check in next

## Chapter 7: The 80/20 of AI Prompts

### The One Prompt Framework
Every good AI prompt has 4 parts:
1. **Role**: "You are a..."
2. **Context**: What the situation is
3. **Task**: What to produce
4. **Format**: How it should look

### The Iteration Loop
Don't write the perfect prompt. Run it 3 times first. AI learns your preference through iteration. The third version is always better than the first draft.

### Prompt Stacking
Chain prompts together: output from Prompt A becomes input for Prompt B.
Example: Triage → Response Draft → Subject Line → Send.

## Chapter 8: What NOT to Automate

Be honest about this. AI handles the mechanical. Humans handle the relationship.

Don't automate:
- Breaking up with a client
- Negotiating big deals
- Emotional conversations
- Anything requiring judgment about people
- Legal or financial advice

Do automate:
- Drafting emails (not sending)
- Sorting information
- Generating first versions
- Scheduling
- Reminders
- Reporting
